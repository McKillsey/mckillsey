Special Orders

Nightclub Warehouse — Are They Worth It?
McKillsey Verdict — Skip Them

We modeled all 54 known special orders against the standard 20-hour sell cycle. Each order was simulated with optimal technician assignment: order goods produced to warehouse capacity, free techs on the highest-value alternatives, dynamic reassignment as techs complete their tasks. All unit prices use the +35% MC-buffed nightclub warehouse values.

No special order beats the standard cycle on both $/passive hr and $/active hr. Eight orders edge out the benchmark on passive rate, but every one of them is dramatically worse on active — the best-paying winner delivers $2.5M/active hr against the benchmark's $5.5M. Meanwhile, the handful of orders with competitive active rates require 40–60 hours to fill, cratering your passive rate by 30% or more.

The remaining 46 orders lose on both metrics. The 5–20% bonus does not compensate for pulling technicians off your top-5 lineup or waiting past the 20-hour mark. Sell your warehouse normally. Ignore special orders entirely.

Methodology
Simulation Details

Each order is simulated minute-by-minute with 5 technicians. Three techs must cover the order's required goods; the remaining two are assigned to the highest $/hr goods not already covered. All techs produce to warehouse capacity — the order withdraws its required units at sell time, and remaining stock is sold separately. When any tech fills their good to warehouse max, they are dynamically reassigned to the next most productive available good.

Special order payouts are recalculated using +35% MC-buffed nightclub warehouse values (Coke $27,000, Meth $11,475, Cash $4,725) with the listed bonus percentage applied. Non-MC goods (Cargo, Sporting, Weed, Forgery) use standard unit values. Remaining stock is sold with Tony's 10% cut capped at $100K. All figures are invite-only. The benchmark is the standard 20-hour top-5 cycle at $45,425/passive hr.

Special order compositions sourced from community documentation (54 known orders). Payouts use +35% MC-buffed nightclub warehouse values — this is the most generous assumption for special orders, and they still lose.
