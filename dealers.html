Street Dealers

Insights · Drug Sales Channel Analysis
McKillsey Verdict
Default Recommendation — Skip

Street dealers exist to tempt you into selling product below what your businesses would earn. In most scenarios, they pay roughly supply cost — meaning you're donating your production time for pennies, or outright losing money on meth and weed. Your MC businesses and Nightclub sell for dramatically more per unit. The only product with a defensible normal-week margin is acid, and even that's modest.

The exception is 2× dealer weeks, covered below, which flip the math enough to be worth your time.

Reading the Table

At normal prices, coke yields pocket change, meth and weed are net negative, and acid is the only product with a real margin. Premium prices make everything profitable — but each dealer only pays premium on one product, and the quantities are too small to build a strategy around.

The Math Is Clear

Even accounting for the chance of premium pricing, MC and Acid Lab sells with HDB earn 2× to 4× more than expected dealer revenue for the same product. Even Nightclub sells — which cost nothing to produce — match or beat dealer prices. Every unit you divert to a dealer is a unit you're selling at a steep discount to what your businesses would earn.

When 2× Dealer Week Changes the Math
2× Week Recommendation — Worth Your Time (with caveats)

During 2× weeks, all dealer prices double — but your MC businesses and Acid Lab still pay more per unit. If you sell your full stock regularly, you're still better off selling through your businesses (or through your Acid Lab with HDB). Dealers don't change that math.

Where 2× dealers shine is spare capacity. If you don't sell the moment you hit full stock — which realistically describes most players — dealers let you monetize production that would otherwise sit idle. Three dealer visits per day takes ~15 minutes of driving for an expected $325,785 in passive-adjacent income on top of your normal rotation.

Players who sell Nightclub only and skip MC sells should sell their coke, meth, and acid to dealers during 2× weeks — but not weed, which still earns more through the Nightclub with HDB.

McKillsey’s 2× Week Take

MC businesses and Acid Lab still pay more per unit — but the gap narrows considerably during 2× weeks. The key difference is effort: dealer sales require ~5 minutes of driving per stop with zero setup, while MC sells require full stock, crew coordination, and sell mission time. During 2× weeks, dealers become a reasonable low-effort source of income — $325K/day for 15 minutes of casual driving on top of your normal rotation.

The standout: Acid at 2× is within 7% of an Acid Lab sell with HDB, making dealers nearly as profitable, though the low quantities of product that dealers buy limits the potential for this strategy.

Dealer Mechanics Reference
Availability
Three dealers spawn daily across Los Santos. Locations rotate. Each buys all four products in fixed quantities: 1 coke, 2 meth, 10 weed, 10 acid per visit.
Premium Product
Each dealer designates one product as premium, paying 2× the normal price for that product only. Probabilities: Cocaine 15%, Meth 20%, Weed 30%, Acid 35%. Premium assignments rotate with dealer locations.
Stock Priority
Dealers pull from MC business stock first. If your MC business is empty for that product, they pull from your Nightclub warehouse instead. You cannot choose which source to sell from.
Production Notes
MC businesses produce the same quantity per supply bar regardless of upgrades — upgrades only affect speed. Acid requires the equipment upgrade to produce more per supply cycle (150 min standard, 90 min boosted).
Methodology
Data Sources & Assumptions

Supply costs assume fully upgraded MC businesses and standard acid lab. Dealer prices are ranges observed in-game — midpoints used for profit calculations where a single figure is shown. MC business sell values are per-unit remote sell prices with full upgrades, no lobby bonus. Nightclub values are per-unit with full equipment upgrade. Per-unit values for the Nightclub column use the standard $/unit without the High Demand Bonus.
